Short Essay Questions

1. Concerning transport of glucose across the cell membrane, study the diagram provided (Intestinal lumen/Enterocyte/Interstitium).

Model Answers

a) Transporters: A = SGLT1 (Sodium-Glucose Linked Transporter), B = Na+/K+ ATPase (Sodium-Potassium Pump), C = GLUT2. Molecules: 1 = Na+ (Sodium), 2 = Glucose, 3 = K+ (Potassium).

b) A = Secondary Active Transport (Symport); B = Primary Active Transport; C = Facilitated Diffusion.

c) Process B directly utilizes ATP to create a gradient; Process A uses the electrochemical gradient established by B; Process C is purely passive and does not require energy.

2. State the changes (Increase/Decrease/No Change) for ECF volume, ECF osmolarity, ICF volume, and ICF osmolarity in the following conditions:

Model Answers

Diarrhea: ECF vol ↓, ICF vol (no change), ECF/ICF osmolarity (no change) - Isotonic loss.

Diabetes Insipidus: ECF vol ↓, ICF vol ↓, ECF/ICF osmolarity ↑ - Pure water loss.

SIADH: ECF vol ↑, ICF vol ↑, ECF/ICF osmolarity ↓ - Pure water gain/dilution.

Adrenal Insufficiency: ECF vol ↓, ICF vol ↑, ECF/ICF osmolarity ↓ - Sodium loss (Hypotonic contraction).

Uremia: ECF vol ↑, ICF vol (no change), ECF/ICF osmolarity ↑ - Accumulation of urea (effective vs ineffective osmoles).
